What is Category Research ?

Category Management is a strategic approach to align organization’ resources to perform focused procurement whereby segmenting organization’ spend into areas which contain similar or related products or services together.

Category Management may involve bundling products into direct and indirect products or services or may buckets products or services further by volume, value, supplier or type. Later, using various associated theories, such as Pareto (80/20 rule), Kraljic matrix, and ABC analysis, fathom insights about these categories purchasing behaviours and help organizations priorities and focus their spending and efforts.

In addition, Category management involves mapping of the supply and logistic lines for products and its raw materials along with generating insights on market and supplier universe. These mapping of the supply chain and subsequent insights are fortified with understanding of social, political and environmental factors and thereby ameliorate knowledge on available supply chain risk and opportunities. For example: Low grass production would lead to drop milk production and subsequently a drop in overall butter produce. This scenario will erode profit margins of hospitality business, where butter is consumed considerably.

What is Trade Facilitation ?

Trade facilitation looks at how procedures and controls governing the movement of goods across national borders can be improved to reduce associated cost burdens and maximise efficiency while safeguarding legitimate regulatory objectives. Business costs may be a direct function of collecting information and submitting declarations or an indirect consequence of border checks in the form of delays and associated time penalties, forgone business opportunities and reduced competitiveness. (Source Wiki)
